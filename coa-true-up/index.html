Handoff in progress

Pull every CoA NCTL has issued out of Confident Cannabis and into NCTL's own archive.

North Coast Testing Laboratories generates Certificates of Analysis and currently depends on Confident Cannabis as the authoritative archive. We're closing that gap: every of-record CoA goes into NCTL's own Azure Files share, with cryptographic integrity and per-file provenance. After this ships, NCTL's internal LIMS becomes the system of record.

This project is independent of the in-flight Kondo branch of the NCLims codebase. It reads production read-only, talks to Confident's API, writes to the existing Azure Files share, and keeps its bookkeeping in its own isolated ledger. No NCLims source-code changes required. Pearson's microbials release is untouched.

Stakeholder decisions — Adam's section

What this section is for

Six product-level decisions about the rollout. Already-recommended (★) defaults shown — most stakeholders can scan, pick the recommended option, and move on. Adam will compile responses and hand David the chosen options separately from the docs above.

David doesn't need these answered before starting the project — the smoke test + ledger work is independent of these calls. Decisions 1, 2, 5 affect the rollout itself; decisions 3, 4, 6 affect scope outside the core flow.

Decision 1 of 6

Which lab gets backfilled first?

The backfill pulls hundreds of thousands of CoAs from Confident — Ohio (NCTL) and Michigan (NCTLM) combined. We can run them concurrently, or stagger them so we validate the pipeline on one lab before pointing it at the other.

Decision 2 of 6

How fast should the backfill run?

Confident has no bulk-download endpoint — we have to ask for each sample one at a time. Roughly 400,000 network calls for the historical corpus. Pace determines wall-clock time and risk of Confident's API rate-limiting us.

Decision 3 of 6

When Confident has multiple CoA files for one sample, what do we do?

Confident's data model holds a primary CoA per sample plus a list called "CoA additions" — additional file references attached to the same sample. We don't yet know empirically what these are: amendment history, supplemental documents (method addendums, accreditation letters), or something else. Sample 217848 (our one documented historical amendment) is our gold test case.

Decision 4 of 6

What about samples that exist in Confident but have no matching LIMS record?

After the backfill imports everything Confident has for samples LIMS knows about, there will likely be CoAs in Confident that don't map to any LIMS sample. Usually side-channel work: 9999-prefixed placeholder manifests, outsourced testing, special-projects samples.

Decision 5 of 6

What happens if someone regenerates a backfilled CoA?

A known hazard: regenerating a historical CoA through today's LIMS code can produce a different result than the as-issued version. Panel definitions, regulatory limits, and analyte names have all changed. Confident's preserved-as-issued bytes are the only source of regulatory truth for older CoAs.

Decision 6 of 6

When does the deferred LIMS-side amendment UX get picked back up?

The amendment-authoring workflow (modal, canned amendment messages, native red "AMENDED" cover-page banner) was pulled from v1 to protect Pearson's microbials release. It's clearly desired — this decision is about scheduling.
