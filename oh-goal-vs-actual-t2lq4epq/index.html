Section 1 of 8 · setup

How "Last Reported" was computed

The canonical "reported out the door" timestamp in the schema is SampleReportingStatus.SentToCcOn — but that table is empty in NCLimsProjectKondo_AA. It's a Kondo-only table that isn't backfilled from prod.

So I used ReportHistory.ReportedOn (the CoA-finalized timestamp) instead. Coverage check: 96.9% of Apr-window OH samples have a ReportHistory row, and it agrees with Sample.ResultsSentToConfidentCannabis = 1 one-for-one. UTC→ET conversion applied before any date comparison. The 17:00 ET "EOD" thresholds were also tested as a separate slice.

You called the field "not immutable but statistically close to perfect" — that matches the ReportHistory.ReportedOn semantics: it's the timestamp the CoA was finalized, which is what customers see land.

Section 6 of 8 · NeededBy strategy

What the current NeededBy strategy gets right

Recap of the current rule (from the bulktat skill): seed SampleAssay.NeededBy from PanelsDue.xlsx; when Jess sends "Micro Goal X / Actual Y" use Actual; chemistry uses the single date as-is; everything stored at 17:00 ET on the deadline date.

  • Coverage is 100% for Apr 13–24. Every OH sample and every SampleAssay row has a NeededBy populated. The /panelsdue daily run is doing its job.
  • "Use Actual, discard Goal" is empirically correct for micro. 68% of micro samples land in the Goal→Actual window; only 10% hit Goal. If we seeded NeededBy = Goal instead, ~90% of micro samples would false-flag as late and the signal would be useless. Setting NeededBy = Actual produces a clean ~12% real late-flag rate.
  • 17:00 ET + ET-local storage matches Jess's "Due-/EOD" semantics. The CLAUDE.md carve-out from the UTC policy is correctly applied here — doing anything else (UTC storage, midnight default) would create timezone bugs in every on-time metric.
Section 7 of 8 · NeededBy gaps

Four ways the current strategy is inadequate

  1. Chemistry treats an aspirational date as authoritative. 72–78% late means "late" stops being a useful signal — it's the default state. Options: (a) ask Jess to send Chemistry Goal+Actual when chem also slips into a non-business day, (b) compute synthetic chem-Actual = chem-stated + 1 business day, or (c) accept the chem-stated as a real customer promise and start measuring against it as a KPI.
  2. The Goal date is thrown away. Goal = the date the customer was promised. Today's CS dashboards score against Actual, so a sample landing on Actual is "green" even though customer expectation was Goal. Worth a two-column model — NeededBy (operational, = Actual, drives Rush flags) and NeededByGoal (informational, surfaced in CS views) — so we can answer "how often did we beat customer expectation?" without losing the operational signal.
  3. No daily Jess-coverage gate. 100% coverage today is real, but if PanelsDue.xlsx ever fails to land (Power Automate hiccup) or Jess uses phrasing the regex misses, samples silently get a zero/default NeededBy and nothing alerts. Worth adding to the /panelsdue summary: "of N OH manifests received yesterday, M parsed a Jess deadline."
  4. "After-Actual" misses are invisible to CS. Innovative Healing's 4/23 batch went ~21h past its 4/28 deadline and nothing currently surfaces that. The Confirmation Tracker / Pre-Release Review framework is the natural home — reported_et > NeededByActual is the trigger event.
