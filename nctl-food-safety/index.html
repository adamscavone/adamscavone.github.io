Should NCTL add a Food Safety Services line?

A strategic-exploration brief for executive review. Prepared by Adam Scavone (NCTL) with AI-assisted analysis. Discussion document only — no commitments, no capex authorized.

What this is

NCTL is a licensed cannabis testing lab serving Ohio and Michigan. The lab currently tests cannabis flower, concentrates, and infused edibles (marijuana-infused granolas, gummies, beverages, baked goods) for cannabinoids, terpenes, pesticides, heavy metals, residual solvents, and microbials.

This brief explores whether NCTL should expand into a Food Safety Services line, anchored by allergen testing (starting with gluten and peanut) for those same cannabis-edibles customers, with possible extensions into on-site sanitation programs, food-safety consulting, and food-fraud / authenticity testing. The original prompt was “gluten test for our customers”; during scoping it expanded into a broader strategic question, which is what this page covers.

Your job as the reader: pick the framing below that matches how you want to engage, read it, then click through the nine decision cards at the bottom of any page. Your answers compile into a markdown block to send back.

How this brief was prepared

The analysis was drafted using Claude (an AI assistant by Anthropic) working from Adam’s scoping prompts. Two additional AI models (Google Gemini and OpenAI Codex) were consulted independently with the same four executive questions to triangulate the recommendation. Their answers appear on the Recommendation page with attribution. Where they agreed, that’s reflected as stronger signal; where they disagreed (phasing of the allergen panel), both views are surfaced and the call is explicit.

This is not a market study. Instrument cost ranges and per-customer ARR estimates come from industry benchmarks and the peer LLM consults — not from customer interviews. Customer-pull validation is one of the nine decisions below.
