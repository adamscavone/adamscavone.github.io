What you’re reading — Page C of 3

This is the decision-only framing of a strategic-exploration brief for NCTL (a licensed cannabis testing lab in Ohio + Michigan). The question: should NCTL add a Food Safety Services line for its cannabis-edibles customers (and possibly conventional food customers)? This page strips out all analysis and shows just the nine decisions that need answers. If you haven’t read either of the other framings, start with the Recommendation page (opinionated, ~10 min) or the Options page (neutral comparison, ~7 min). The Overview page explains how this brief was prepared.

Nothing here commits NCTL to anything. No capex authorized, no customer interviews done yet. Click your answers; Compile decisions in the bottom-right gives you a markdown block to send back to Adam.

Decision 1 of 9

Posture

Do we build the Food Safety Services line, or not?

Decision 2 of 9

Capex authorization

One microplate reader runs every allergen kit. Entry capex sized for that reality.

Decision 3 of 9

Analytical method for allergen testing

ELISA is the FDA-recognized regulatory standard (AOAC 2012.01 for gluten). LC-MS/MS exists but its AOAC method (2017.17) is First Action only, in limited matrices.

Decision 4 of 9

Services scope

How far up the value chain do we go? Each step adds ARR per customer but also delivery complexity.

Decision 5 of 9 · peer LLMs disagreed

Allergen panel phasing

This is the only call where Gemini and Codex split. Gemini argued Big 9 day-one (“producers won’t split samples between labs”). Codex argued gluten+peanut MVP (“validation burden multiplies before you know which allergens produce demand”).

Decision 6 of 9

Authenticity beachhead

If food-fraud / authenticity testing is in scope, where does it start? Both peer LLMs agreed on cannabis-product first.

Decision 7 of 9

Demand validation

Do we survey customers before greenlighting capex, or proceed on conviction?

Decision 8 of 9

Staffing for on-site / consulting

Decision 9 of 9

Branding
